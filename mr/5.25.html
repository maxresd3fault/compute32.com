OVERVIEW

The PowerEdge R730XD is a great choice for an affordable storage server. This machine is similar to what we looked at last month, whereas the listing last month focused much more on compute power while this server focuses more on storage expandability. This machine has 12 hot-swappable 2.5" SATA/SAS hybrid drive bays and comes configured with two Xeon E5-2620 processors for a total of 16 cores, making it still capable of certain compute heavy tasks.

Keep in mind before buying this specific listing: this Dell only comes with two of the 12 drive trays. If you plan to put in more than two drives you will need to source the trays elsewhere. But considering the cheap price of the machine, this shouldn't be a deal breaker.

This machine would make a great file server, and it still comes with enough CPU and RAM to be used for other tasks besides just storage.

For the price, this Dell is yet again a great value. As I said last month, I find Dell to make reliable and easily serviceable hardware. Furthermore Dell provides great support for their older products by continuing to host drivers, BIOS updates, and manuals. The eBay seller (ServerSuperStore) also includes a 30 day warranty.
